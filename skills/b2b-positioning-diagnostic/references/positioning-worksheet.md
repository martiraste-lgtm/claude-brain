## Positioning Worksheet

Use this worksheet to guide the user through structured positioning input, one section at a time. Gather answers before moving to templates.

### Part 1: Understand the Market

**Target ICP**
Who is the ideal customer? Who's facing the challenge that the product solves?

**Use Case (Job-to-be-done)**
What do they want to do/achieve?

**Core Problem**
Why is this difficult? What strategic or operational pain exists, regardless of the solution?

**Alternative Solutions**
What kinds of alternatives exist? Build clusters:
- Cluster 1: ...
- Cluster 2: ...
- Cluster 3: ...

**Dominant Current Solution (Status Quo)**
Which of the alternative solutions is the dominant way? How do they currently try to solve it?

**3 Key Limitations of Status Quo**
Why doesn't their current approach solve the problem properly?
1. ...
2. ...
3. ...

### Part 2: About You (Your positioning in the market)

**Primary Anchor** (choose one — see `references/anchor-framework.md` for decision tree)
- [ ] Activity: What manual task they currently do?
- [ ] Use Case: What is the job-to-be-done?
- [ ] Product Category: What recognized software category?
- [ ] Competitive Alternative: Which competitor?

**Secondary Angle(s)** — max 2 differentiators (see `references/anchor-framework.md`)
- [ ] Niche Down (Who exactly?)
- [ ] Low-Cost Player (Compared to what?)
- [ ] Premium Player (What makes you superior?)
- [ ] Unique Attribute (What is ONLY true for you?)
- [ ] Lite Version (What complexity are you removing?)

**Competitive Differentiator**
Unlike [dominant current solution], we ... [competitive differentiator]

## Strategic Positioning Statement Templates (Internal)

These are internal narratives (3-5 sentences) that justify why you exist. Not marketing copy — strategic foundation.

Choose the template matching your Primary Anchor.

### Activity Positioning (Low market maturity)

**Template:**
> [ICP] struggle with [CORE PROBLEM]. Today, they handle this by [MANUAL WORKFLOW]. This creates key limitations: 1. [LIMITATION 1], 2. [LIMITATION 2], 3. [LIMITATION 3]. We built [SOLUTION FRAMED AROUND THE ACTIVITY] so that [ICP] can [OUTCOME]. Unlike manual processes, we [UNIQUE DIFFERENTIATOR].

**Example (CRM):**
> Early-stage SaaS founders struggle with losing visibility into their sales pipeline as deal volume increases. Today, they track prospects using spreadsheets, notes, and scattered tools. This creates key limitations: 1. No centralized overview, 2. Inconsistent follow-ups, 3. Lost context across conversations. We built a structured way for founders to track all customer information in one place so they can stay organized. Unlike manual processes, we provide centralized visibility and automated tracking.

### Use Case Positioning (Emerging market maturity)

**Template:**
> [ICP] face the challenge of [CORE PROBLEM]. They attempt to solve this by [FRAGMENTED TOOLS/WORKFLOWS]. This leads to key limitations: 1. [LIMITATION 1], 2. [LIMITATION 2], 3. [LIMITATION 3]. We help [ICP] [JOB-TO-BE-DONE] without these limitations. We are differentiated because we [UNIQUE DIFFERENTIATOR].

**Example (CRM):**
> Early-stage SaaS founders struggle with maintaining visibility and consistency in their sales pipeline. They attempt to manage prospects across spreadsheets, Slack, and email threads. This leads to key limitations: 1. Fragmented information, 2. Missed follow-ups, 3. Lack of structured tracking. We help early-stage SaaS founders manage their prospects without juggling scattered tools. We provide a lightweight system designed specifically for founders.

### Product Category Positioning (Established market maturity)

**Template:**
> [ICP] face the problem of [CORE PROBLEM]. They evaluate [PRODUCT CATEGORY] solutions to address it. However, most category players create limitations: 1. [LIMITATION 1], 2. [LIMITATION 2], 3. [LIMITATION 3]. We built a [PRODUCT CATEGORY] specifically for [ICP]. Unlike typical category tools, we [COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATOR].

**Example (CRM):**
> Early-stage SaaS founders struggle to manage and scale their sales pipeline effectively. They evaluate CRM software to address this challenge. Most CRM tools are not made for early stage founders: 1. Complex onboarding, 2. Feature overload, 3. High pricing. We built a CRM software specifically for early-stage SaaS founders. Unlike enterprise-focused CRMs, we offer a lightweight and easy-to-use solution tailored to startups.

### Competitive Alternative Positioning (High market maturity)

**Template:**
> [ICP] face the problem of [CORE PROBLEM]. Many rely on [MARKET LEADER] to address it. However, this creates limitations: 1. [LIMITATION 1], 2. [LIMITATION 2], 3. [LIMITATION 3]. We are the [CLEAR IMPROVEMENT ADJECTIVE] alternative to [MARKET LEADER] built for [ICP]. Unlike [MARKET LEADER], we [COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATOR].

**Example (CRM):**
> Early-stage SaaS founders struggle to manage their growing sales pipeline efficiently. Many rely on HubSpot to address this challenge. However, HubSpot comes with a few limitations for founders: 1. Overwhelming feature sets, 2. Long setup times, 3. Enterprise pricing. We are the lightweight alternative to HubSpot built for early-stage SaaS founders. Unlike HubSpot, we offer fast setup, simplicity, and startup-friendly pricing.

## One-Sentence Positioning Statement Templates (Market-Facing)

These are compressed, market-facing anchors. Choose based on your primary positioning anchor.

### Activity Positioning

**Template:** We help **[ICP] [manual activity they currently do]** so they can **[outcome]**.

**Example:** We help B2B SaaS founders track all their customer information in one place so they never lose deal context.

### Use Case Positioning

**Template:** We help **[ICP] [clear job-to-be-done]** without **[limitations of the status quo]**.

Optional addition: Using **[unique differentiator]**.

**Example:** We help B2B SaaS founders manage their prospects without juggling spreadsheets and scattered notes.

### Product Category Positioning

**Template (A):** We are a **[product category]** for **[specific ICP]**.

**Template (B):** We are a **[product category]** with **[unique differentiator]**.

**Example:** We are a CRM software for early-stage SaaS founders. / We are a lightweight CRM built for startups.

### Competitive Alternative Positioning

**Template (Simple):** We are a **[product category]** alternative to **[leader]** built for **[ICP]** with **[unique differentiator]**.

**Template (Advanced):** We are the **[clear improvement adjective] alternative to [leader]** for **[ICP]** who want **[clear outcome or advantage]**.

**Example:** We are a CRM software alternative to HubSpot built for early-stage SaaS founders with simpler setup. / We're the lightweight alternative to HubSpot for early-stage SaaS founders who want to manage their pipeline without enterprise complexity.

## Positioning Health Check

Before moving on to messaging, pressure-test the positioning. If the user can't confidently answer "Yes" to most of these, go back and refine.

**1. Can a buyer immediately understand what you are?**
If not → Primary Anchor may be unclear.

**2. Is it obvious what you replace?**
If not → Define competitive alternatives more clearly.

**3. Is your differentiation meaningful?**
- Would a competitor struggle to copy this?
- Is it more than a vague statement ("better," "simpler," "more powerful")?
If not → Define your secondary anchor (differentiators) more sharply.
