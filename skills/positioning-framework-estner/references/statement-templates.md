# Statement Templates — Strategic & One-Sentence Positioning

## Due tipi di statement

Il processo di positioning produce DUE artefatti testuali:

1. **Strategic Positioning Statement (Internal)** — la narrativa che giustifica perché il prodotto esiste. 3-5 frasi. Non è copy marketing, è il documento strategico interno.

2. **One-Sentence Positioning Statement** — la versione market-facing compressa. Una frase. Questo è l'anchor che entra in homepage, deck, sales pitch.

I template cambiano in base al **Primary Anchor scelto**. Ogni anchor ha il suo template dedicato.

---

## PARTE 1 — Strategic Positioning Statement (Internal Version)

Questi template producono il documento strategico interno. 3-5 frasi. Scrivilo come narrativa, non come slogan.

### Template 1 — Activity Positioning

```
[ICP] struggle with [Core Problem].
Today, they handle this by [manual workflow].

This creates key limitations:
• [Limitation 1]
• [Limitation 2]
• [Limitation 3]

We built [solution framed around the activity] so that [ICP] can [outcome].
Unlike manual processes, we [unique differentiator].
```

**Esempio (CRM)**:
```
Early-stage SaaS founders struggle with losing visibility into their sales pipeline as deal volume increases.
Today, they track prospects using spreadsheets, notes, and scattered tools.

This creates key limitations:
• No centralized overview
• Inconsistent follow-ups
• Lost context across conversations

We built a structured way for founders to track all customer information in one place so they can stay organized.
Unlike manual processes, we provide centralized visibility and automated tracking.
```

---

### Template 2 — Use Case Positioning

```
[ICP] face the challenge of [Core Problem].
They attempt to solve this by [fragmented tools/workflows].

This leads to key limitations:
• [Limitation 1]
• [Limitation 2]
• [Limitation 3]

We help [ICP] [job-to-be-done] without these limitations.
We are differentiated because we [unique differentiator].
```

**Esempio (CRM)**:
```
Early-stage SaaS founders struggle with maintaining visibility and consistency in their sales pipeline.
They attempt to manage prospects across spreadsheets, Slack, and email threads.

This leads to key limitations:
• Fragmented information
• Missed follow-ups
• Lack of structured tracking

We help early-stage SaaS founders manage their prospects without juggling scattered tools.
We provide a lightweight system designed specifically for founders.
```

---

### Template 3 — Product Category Positioning

```
[ICP] face the problem of [Core Problem].
They evaluate [Product Category] solutions to address it.

However, most category players create limitations:
• [Limitation 1]
• [Limitation 2]
• [Limitation 3]

We built a [Product Category] specifically for [ICP].
Unlike typical category tools, we [competitive differentiator].
```

**Esempio (CRM)**:
```
Early-stage SaaS founders struggle to manage and scale their sales pipeline effectively.
They evaluate CRM software to address this challenge.

Most CRM tools are not made for early-stage founders:
• Complex onboarding
• Feature overload
• High pricing

We built a CRM software specifically for early-stage SaaS founders.
Unlike enterprise-focused CRMs, we offer a lightweight and easy-to-use solution tailored to startups.
```

---

### Template 4 — Competitive Alternative Positioning

```
[ICP] face the problem of [Core Problem].
Many rely on [Market Leader] to address it.

However, this creates limitations:
• [Limitation 1]
• [Limitation 2]
• [Limitation 3]

We are the [clear improvement adjective] alternative to [Market Leader] built for [ICP].
Unlike [Market Leader], we [competitive differentiator].
```

**Esempio (CRM)**:
```
Early-stage SaaS founders struggle to manage their growing sales pipeline efficiently.
Many rely on HubSpot to address this challenge.

However, HubSpot comes with a few limitations for founders:
• Overwhelming feature sets
• Long setup times
• Enterprise pricing

We are the lightweight alternative to HubSpot built for early-stage SaaS founders.
Unlike HubSpot, we offer fast setup, simplicity, and startup-friendly pricing.
```

## PARTE 2 — One-Sentence Positioning Statement

Questa è la versione market-facing compressa. Usa il template corrispondente al tuo Primary Anchor.

### 1️⃣ Activity Positioning

**Template**:
```
We help [ICP] [manual activity they currently do] so they can [outcome].
```

**Esempio**:
> We help B2B SaaS founders track all their customer information in one place so they never lose deal context.

**Quando usarlo bene**:
- Thought leadership, LinkedIn content
- Spiegare al non-buyer cosa fai
- Pagine di educazione ("what is...") del sito

---

### 2️⃣ Use Case Positioning

**Template base**:
```
We help [ICP] [clear job-to-be-done] without [limitations of the status quo].
```

**Template esteso (con Unique Attribute)**:
```
We help [ICP] [clear job-to-be-done] without [limitations of the status quo].
Using [unique differentiator].
```

**Esempio**:
> We help B2B SaaS founders manage their prospects without juggling spreadsheets and scattered notes.

**Quando usarlo bene**:
- Homepage (Hero section)
- Sales deck opening slide
- Outbound email opening line
- About us page

---

### 3️⃣ Product Category Positioning

**Template 1 — Semplice**:
```
We are a [product category] for [specific ICP].
```

**Template 2 — Con Unique Attribute**:
```
We are a [product category] with [unique differentiator].
```

**Esempi**:
> We are a CRM software for early-stage SaaS founders.
>
> We are a lightweight CRM built for startups.

**Quando usarlo bene**:
- G2/Capterra profile
- SEO title pages ("What is [category]?")
- Paid search landing pages (category-keyword traffic)
- LinkedIn company profile

---

### 4️⃣ Competitive Alternative Positioning

**Template semplice**:
```
We are a [product category] alternative to [leader] built for [ICP] with [unique differentiator].
```

**Template avanzato**:
```
We are the [clear improvement adjective] alternative to [leader] for [ICP] who want [clear outcome or advantage].
```

**Esempi**:
> We are a CRM software alternative to HubSpot built for early-stage SaaS founders with simpler setup.
>
> We're the lightweight alternative to HubSpot for early-stage SaaS founders who want to manage their pipeline without enterprise complexity.

**Quando usarlo bene**:
- Comparison pages ("X vs Leader")
- Paid search on competitor keywords
- Outbound targeting leader's customers
- Case study titles ("Why we switched from X")

## Come produrre gli statement nel workflow

### Step 1 — Raccogli gli input dalla positioning-worksheet.md
Hai bisogno di:
- ICP
- Core Problem
- Status Quo / Market Leader / Category
- 3 Key Limitations
- Primary Anchor (già scelto)
- Secondary Angle(s) (già scelti, max 2)
- Unique Differentiator / Competitive Differentiator

### Step 2 — Scrivi lo Strategic Positioning Statement
Usa il template corrispondente al Primary Anchor. 3-5 frasi. È il documento interno.

### Step 3 — Comprimi in One-Sentence Statement
Usa il template corrispondente. Una frase, market-facing.

### Step 4 — Genera 2-3 varianti
Scrivi almeno 2-3 varianti dello One-Sentence. Proponi all'utente tutte le varianti e fagli scegliere quella che risuona di più con il buyer reale (non con se stesso).

### Step 5 — Test di chiarezza
Leggi la frase ad alta voce. Se il tuo partner (che non conosce la tua azienda) capisce cosa fai in 10 secondi, passa. Altrimenti, rifai.

## Common Mistakes negli statement

### Mistake 1: Usare il template di un anchor diverso
Se hai scelto Use Case, non usare il template Competitive Alternative. Il template è coerente con l'anchor per una ragione.

### Mistake 2: Comprimere 3 angle in una frase
*"We help startup founders manage their prospects without juggling spreadsheets, with AI-powered insights, using native WhatsApp integration."* — troppo denso. Max 1 angle nello statement one-sentence.

### Mistake 3: Verbo vago
"Empower", "enable", "unlock", "transform" → parole vuote. Il buyer non sa cosa farai concretamente. Usa verbi operativi: track, manage, schedule, build, replace.

### Mistake 4: Lingua del founder, non del buyer
Se il buyer non usa parole come "stack", "workflow", "orchestration", non metterle nello statement. Usa le sue parole.

### Mistake 5: Troppo clever
"We make [category] fun again" / "[category] reimagined" / "The future of [category]" → slogan, non positioning. Il buyer non capisce cosa stai facendo. Clever > clear è sempre un errore.
