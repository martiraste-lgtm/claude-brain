# Campaign Benchmarks Reference

This file is a reference for the `demand-gen-campaign-brief` skill. Use it to sanity-check KPI targets, set realistic channel-level expectations, and calibrate budget recommendations. All benchmarks include source citations. Use ranges, not point estimates, when briefing stakeholders — performance varies significantly by industry, list quality, offer, and creative execution.

---

## Email Marketing Benchmarks

### Overall Email Performance by Industry (B2B)

| Industry | Avg Open Rate | Avg CTR | Avg Unsubscribe Rate |
|----------|-------------|---------|---------------------|
| Marketing & Advertising | 17–22% | 2–3% | 0.3–0.5% |
| Software / SaaS | 20–28% | 2–4% | 0.2–0.4% |
| IT & Technology | 18–25% | 2–4% | 0.2–0.4% |
| Financial Services | 20–27% | 3–5% | 0.2–0.4% |
| Healthcare | 22–30% | 3–5% | 0.2–0.3% |
| Professional Services | 22–32% | 3–6% | 0.2–0.4% |
| Education | 22–30% | 3–5% | 0.2–0.4% |
| Nonprofits | 25–35% | 3–6% | 0.1–0.3% |

*(Sources: HubSpot Email Marketing Benchmarks; Mailchimp Email Marketing Statistics by Industry)*

**Important context:** Open rate benchmarks shifted significantly after Apple's Mail Privacy Protection (MPP) launched in 2021, which inflates open rates for Apple Mail users. If your MAP reports unusually high open rates (45%+), validate against click rates rather than opens. Click-to-open rate (CTOR) is a more reliable engagement metric in MPP-affected environments.

### Email Performance by Campaign Type

| Campaign Type | Typical Open Rate | Typical CTR | Notes |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------|-------|
| Welcome email (single send) | 45–65% | 8–15% | Highest open rates of any email type |
| Nurture sequence (email 1) | 30–45% | 4–8% | Drops by ~30% per subsequent email |
| Nurture sequence (email 2-3) | 20–30% | 3–6% | Consistent engagement signals good list health |
| Promotional / campaign blast | 15–25% | 2–4% | Frequency-sensitive; diminishing returns above 2x/week |
| Re-engagement / winback | 10–20% | 1–3% | Lower rates expected; segment and sunset non-openers |
| Transactional / triggered | 40–60% | 8–20% | Behavior-triggered emails dramatically outperform batch |
| Cold outbound (well-targeted) | 25–45% | 3–8% | Highly dependent on list quality and personalization |

*(Sources: Mailchimp Email Marketing Benchmarks; Campaign Monitor Email Marketing Benchmarks; HubSpot State of Marketing)*

### Email Conversion Rate Benchmarks

- **Email to landing page conversion (B2B offer):** 1–5% of email sends; well-optimized campaigns with relevant offers achieve 5–10%
- **Email nurture sequence to MQL conversion:** Ranges from 3–8% of sequence starters who become MQLs *(Source: Marketo/Adobe Engagement Platform benchmarks)*
- **Optimal send frequency:** Most B2B audiences tolerate 1–2 emails per week from a trusted brand; more than 3x/week correlates with elevated unsubscribe rates *(Source: HubSpot State of Marketing)*
- **Subject line length:** Subject lines under 50 characters perform better on mobile; personalized subject lines improve open rates by 10–20% on average *(Source: Mailchimp, Campaign Monitor)*

## Paid Channel Benchmarks

### LinkedIn Ads

| Metric | Benchmark Range | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Average CTR (all formats) | 0.4–0.6% | Low CTR is normal; focus on CPL, not CTR alone |
| Sponsored Content CTR | 0.35–0.60% | Image ads; video ads often lower CTR but higher view-through |
| Lead Gen Form (LGF) conversion rate | 10–15% | Higher than landing page due to pre-filled form data |
| Landing page conversion rate | 2–6% | Lower than LGF; requires strong LP optimization |
| Average CPL (B2B, all industries) | $75–$200+ | Highly variable; enterprise tech can reach $300+ CPL |
| Average CPM | $30–$80 | Among the highest CPMs of any social platform |
| Cost per click (CPC) | $5–$15 | Wide range based on audience competition |

*(Sources: LinkedIn Marketing Solutions; HubSpot State of Marketing; WordStream LinkedIn Ads Benchmarks)*

**LinkedIn CPL Context:** LinkedIn's high CPL is offset by audience quality. When calculating LinkedIn ROI, compare CPL against average deal size, not against cheaper channels. For enterprise B2B with $50K+ ACV, a $150 CPL that converts at a higher SQL rate can outperform a $30 CPL from a lower-intent channel.

### Google Ads (Search)

| Metric | Branded Search | Non-Branded / Category | Competitor Terms |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Average CTR | 5–15% | 2–5% | 2–4% |
| Average CPC | $1–$5 | $10–$50+ | $15–$60+ |
| Average conversion rate (to lead) | 10–25% | 3–8% | 2–6% |
| Average CPL | $10–$40 | $50–$200+ | $80–$300+ |

*(Sources: WordStream Google Ads Industry Benchmarks; Search Engine Land; HubSpot)*

**Note on non-branded CPL ranges:** Technology and software have some of the highest CPCs in Google Search, with category keywords running $15–$50+ per click. Legal, financial, and insurance are the highest-CPC categories ($50–$100+). CPL varies by landing page quality — a 5% conversion rate vs. 10% halves the CPL with no change in media spend.

### Meta / Facebook Ads (B2B Context)

| Metric | Benchmark Range | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| Average CTR (all placements) | 0.5–1.5% | Feed placements outperform Stories for lead gen |
| Average CPM | $10–$30 | Lower than LinkedIn; broader audience |
| Average CPL (B2B lead gen) | $20–$80 | Much lower than LinkedIn but lower intent audiences |
| Conversion rate (to lead) | 2–5% | Via landing page; Lead Ads forms convert higher |
| Lead Ads form conversion rate | 8–15% | Pre-filled forms reduce friction |

*(Sources: WordStream Facebook Ads Benchmarks; Hootsuite Social Media Trends; AdEspresso Facebook Ads Study)*

**B2B Meta caveat:** Meta's targeting for B2B is weaker than LinkedIn's because job title and company size data are less reliable. Works best for SMB audiences, brand awareness, and retargeting warm audiences from content or website visits. Avoid cold B2B prospecting on Meta for enterprise audiences.

## Landing Page Conversion Benchmarks

| Page Type | Average Conversion Rate | Top Quartile | Notes |
|-----------|------------------------|-------------|-------|
| B2B lead gen landing page (any form) | 2–5% | 10%+ | Top performers use radical focus: one offer, one CTA |
| Gated content download | 20–40% | 50%+ | Higher when content is highly specific and relevant |
| Webinar registration page | 20–40% | 50%+ | Urgency and social proof drive registration rates |
| Free trial sign-up | 2–10% | 15%+ | Friction in the trial flow reduces eventual activation |
| Demo request page | 3–8% | 15%+ | Social proof (logos, reviews) is the highest-leverage element |
| Pricing page to CTA | 5–15% | 20%+ | High intent; optimize ruthlessly — this is a money page |
| Paid ad landing page (cold traffic) | 1–4% | 8%+ | Cold traffic converts lower; align ad promise to page |

*(Sources: HubSpot Marketing Statistics; WordStream Conversion Rate Benchmarks; Unbounce Conversion Benchmark Report)*

**Key landing page levers (in order of impact):**
1. Message match between ad and page (biggest single driver of conversion rate)
2. Single, specific offer with a clear benefit headline
3. Social proof (customer logos, review count, case study stat)
4. Form length — every additional field reduces conversion by 10–20%
5. Page load speed — pages over 3 seconds to load lose 50%+ of mobile visitors *(Source: Google/SOASTA research)*

## Campaign Duration Guidelines

### By Campaign Type

| Campaign Type | Minimum Duration | Recommended Duration | Notes |
|--------------|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| Product launch | 2–4 weeks | 4–8 weeks | Include pre-launch teaser phase if audience is warm |
| Event promotion | 6–8 weeks out | 10–12 weeks out | Longer for in-person events; registration peaks 2-3 weeks before |
| Quarterly lead gen | 10–13 weeks | Full quarter | Build and optimize in first 3-4 weeks; scale in weeks 5-10 |
| Always-on / continuous | Ongoing | Review quarterly | Never fully paused; optimize monthly |
| Re-engagement / winback | 3–6 weeks | 4–8 weeks | Longer sequences have higher recovery rates with non-intrusive cadence |
| Content-led / SEO | 3–6 months to see results | 12+ months to compound | Not a campaign in the traditional sense — requires sustained investment |

**Paid channel optimization timelines:**
- LinkedIn and Google campaigns need 2-4 weeks of data before the algorithm optimizes delivery
- A/B tests need at least 100-200 conversions per variant for statistical significance — factor this into timeline when testing landing pages or ad creative
- Do not pause or make major changes to paid campaigns within the first 2 weeks — disrupts learning phase

## Budget Allocation Models

### The 70/20/10 Model (Proven Channels / Emerging / Experimental)

A widely cited framework for portfolio budget management:
- **70%** allocated to proven channels and tactics with a track record of performance for your business
- **20%** allocated to emerging channels or new formats you have evidence might work
- **10%** allocated to experimental tests with no prior performance data

This prevents both over-indexing on yesterday's winners and spreading budget too thin across unproven tactics.

*(Reference: Often attributed to Google's own marketing philosophy; widely adopted in demand gen)*

### Channel Allocation by Campaign Goal

| Campaign Goal | Paid Social % | Paid Search % | Email % | Content/SEO % | Events % | Other % |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|---------|--------------|----------|---------|
| Brand awareness | 40–50% | 10–15% | 10–15% | 15–25% | 10–20% | 5–10% |
| Lead generation | 25–35% | 25–35% | 15–25% | 10–15% | 5–10% | 5–10% |
| Product launch | 30–40% | 15–25% | 20–30% | 10–15% | 10–20% | 5–10% |
| Event promotion | 25–35% | 10–15% | 25–35% | 5–10% | 10–15% | 5–15% |
| Expansion / upsell | 5–10% | 5–10% | 40–60% | 10–20% | 10–20% | 5–10% |

**Interpretation:** These are starting-point ranges, not prescriptions. Adjust based on your audience's channel behavior, your existing content assets, and historical channel performance. Always shift budget toward what is performing within the first 30 days of a campaign.

### Budget by Company Stage / Campaign Size

| Budget Tier | Monthly Spend | Recommended Channel Focus |
|-------------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Lean | Under $5K/mo | 1-2 channels maximum; email + one paid channel |
| Startup | $5K–$20K/mo | 2-3 channels; paid social + email + content |
| Growth | $20K–$75K/mo | 3-4 channels; can add retargeting, webinars |
| Scale | $75K–$250K/mo | Full channel mix with segmented audiences |
| Enterprise | $250K+/mo | Full-funnel with ABM, events, partner programs |

A common lean budget mistake: spreading $10K across 6 channels at ~$1,600 each. At that spend, no channel has enough data or reach to optimize. Concentrate into 2 channels and learn what works before diversifying.

## Additional Reference Benchmarks

### Content Marketing Performance

- B2B companies that blog produce 67% more leads per month than those that do not *(Source: Demand Metric / HubSpot)*
- Long-form content (1,500+ words) earns 3x more backlinks and 2x more shares than short-form *(Source: SEMrush Content Marketing Study)*
- Organic search drives 53% of all website traffic on average; paid drives 15% *(Source: BrightEdge Research)*
- The average SEO-optimized blog post takes 3–6 months to rank on page 1; highly competitive topics may take 12+ months *(Source: Ahrefs, HubSpot)*

### Webinar / Virtual Event Benchmarks

| Metric | Average | Notes |
|--------|---------|-------|
| Email-to-registration rate | 15–30% | Well-targeted list with relevant topic |
| Registration-to-attendee show rate | 30–45% | Industry average; B2B SaaS often lower |
| Attendee-to-MQL conversion | 30–50% | If registration = MQL, this is post-event to SQL |
| Best day to host webinar | Tuesday–Thursday | Monday and Friday underperform |
| Best start time | 10 AM or 2 PM local time | Accommodates most time zones in a single region |
| Optimal webinar length | 45–60 minutes | 30 min often feels rushed; 90 min loses audience |

*(Sources: ON24 Webinar Benchmarks Report; GoTo Webinar Benchmarks Report; HubSpot)*

### Brand Awareness / Upper Funnel Benchmarks

- Aided brand awareness typically increases by 2–5 percentage points per quarter for consistent TOFU spenders in a defined market *(Source: Nielsen Brand Effect studies)*
- It takes an average of 5–7 brand impressions for a prospect to recall a brand *(widely cited; attributed to various marketing psychology research)*
- Share of voice (SOV) correlates with market share growth: brands with SOV above their market share tend to grow, and vice versa *(Source: Ehrenberg-Bass Institute / Les Binet & Peter Field "The Long and Short of It")*

## How to Use These Benchmarks in a Brief

1. **Set targets as ranges, not point estimates.** "We are targeting 150–200 MQLs at a CPL of $75–$100" is a better brief than "200 MQLs at $80 CPL." Ranges reflect real uncertainty and reduce stakeholder disappointment from single-number misses.

2. **Cite your source when you use a benchmark.** If you set a 25% MQL-to-SQL target because industry average is 13–20%, say so. It shows the target is ambitious but grounded.

3. **Segment benchmarks to your context.** An enterprise-focused campaign should not use SMB SaaS funnel conversion rates. Match the benchmark tier to your actual deal motion.

4. **Establish a baseline before setting targets.** Benchmarks are industry averages. Your own historical data is always more predictive. Use benchmarks only when you lack internal data.

5. **Flag when a stakeholder's target violates benchmarks.** If someone wants a $5 CPL on LinkedIn, the briefing process is the right time to recalibrate — not after the campaign launches.
