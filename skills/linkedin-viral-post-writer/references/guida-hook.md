# LinkedIn Hook Templates & Examples

This reference contains proven hook templates and real examples from viral posts. Use these as inspiration but always create unique combinations.

## High-Performance Hook Templates

### Template 1: Learning Journey
**Format**: "If I had to learn [X] again, I would start here"

**Why it works**:
- Promises efficiency and shortcuts
- Appeals to both beginners and experienced learners
- Creates curiosity about the method

**Variations**:
- "If I could restart my [career/journey] in [field], here's what I'd do"
- "The [X] roadmap I wish I had when starting out"
- "Starting over in [field]? Here's your advantage"

### Template 2: Value + Free Hook
**Format**: "[Dollar amount or impressive metric] [thing] completely free"

**Why it works**:
- Immediate value proposition
- Creates urgency and curiosity
- Appeals to audience desire for resources

**Variations**:
- "$[X] course materials, zero cost"
- "[Number] resources that cost me $[X] to learn, free for you"
- "This [$X value thing] changed my [outcome]—sharing it free"

### Template 3: Meet the Unlikely Hero
**Format**: "Meet [description]: [impressive achievement with specific numbers]"

**Why it works**:
- Storytelling trigger
- Specific details create credibility
- Often includes emotional elements

**Example**: "Meet America's first hot sauce billionaire: a Vietnamese refugee who built a $150M per year empire with no marketing or sales team"

**Variations**:
- "Meet the [age]-year-old who [achievement]"
- "This [unexpected descriptor] [person/company] [achievement]"
- "[Person] went from [struggle] to [success metric]"

### Template 4: Contrarian Take
**Format**: "Everyone tells you [common advice]. They're wrong."

**Why it works**:
- Creates controversy and engagement
- Challenges assumptions
- Makes reader want to know the alternative

**Variations**:
- "The [X] advice that's actually hurting your growth"
- "Stop [common practice]. Do this instead."
- "[Number] rules everyone follows. [Number] are completely wrong."

### Template 5: The Deconstructed Success
**Format**: "I spent [time/money] analyzing [X]. Here's what actually works:"

**Why it works**:
- Shows investment and authority
- Promises practical insights
- Creates anticipation for findings

**Variations**:
- "[Number] hours studying [X] so you don't have to"
- "I analyzed [impressive number] of [X]. The pattern is clear:"
- "After [X attempts/years], here's the only thing that matters"

### Template 6: The Transformation Promise
**Format**: "[Starting point] → [End point]. Here's exactly how:"

**Why it works**:
- Visual appeal with arrow
- Clear before/after
- Promises specific methodology

**Variations**:
- "From [struggle] to [achievement] in [timeframe]"
- "[Metric] to [better metric]: The [X] framework"
- "How we went from [negative] to [positive]"

### Template 7: The Insider Reveal
**Format**: "After [time] in [industry/role], here's what nobody tells you about [X]"

**Why it works**:
- Authority through experience
- Promise of hidden knowledge
- Appeals to curiosity

**Variations**:
- "What [number] years in [field] taught me about [topic]"
- "The [industry] secret that [outcome]"
- "Behind the scenes of [achievement]: The truth"

### Template 8: The Numbers Game
**Format**: "[Impressive number] [thing achieved]. Here's the breakdown:"

**Why it works**:
- Specificity builds credibility
- Numbers attract attention
- Promises tactical insights

**Variations**:
- "[X] in [timeframe]—here's the strategy"
- "From [number] to [bigger number]: The [X] method"
- "[Metric] [number]. Here's what worked:"

## Hook Combination Strategy

**Best viral posts combine 2-3 templates**:

Example combination (Templates 1 + 2):
"If I had to learn Product Management again, I would start here. ($2,000 of resources, completely free)"

Example combination (Templates 3 + 8):
"Meet the founder who built a $5M ARR SaaS with zero employees. 100% remote, 100% automated. Here's the system:"

## Character Count Optimization

**Line 1-2 (62 characters each)**:
- Count includes spaces
- Test in LinkedIn preview
- Front-load the most intriguing element
- Can wrap to line 3 if needed

**Line 3 (50 characters)**:
- Use for additional hook or re-hook
- Creates completeness
- Optional but recommended

**Total hook space**: ~174 characters

## Hook Testing Checklist

Before finalizing a hook, verify:
- [ ] Creates immediate curiosity
- [ ] Includes specific numbers or details (when applicable)
- [ ] Unique combination not seen before
- [ ] Appropriate length for the story
- [ ] Renders well in feed preview
- [ ] Aligns with post content
- [ ] Appeals to target audience emotions

## Common Hook Mistakes

**Avoid these patterns**:
1. **Too generic**: "Leadership lessons I learned"
   - Better: "10 years leading tech teams taught me leadership is [specific insight]"

2. **No specificity**: "How I grew my business"
   - Better: "From $0 to $500K ARR in 8 months—here's the growth playbook"

3. **Buried lede**: "Some thoughts on productivity..."
   - Better: "This productivity system saved me 20 hours per week"

4. **Overused template**: "7 things successful people do"
   - Better: "I tracked 100 founders' routines. Only 3 habits mattered:"

5. **No emotional appeal**: "Data analysis results"
   - Better: "This data analysis revealed why 80% of startups fail (and how to be the 20%)"

## Industry-Specific Hook Patterns

### Tech/SaaS
- Metric-driven: "From [low ARR] to [high ARR]"
- Technical wins: "How we [solved hard problem]"
- Scale stories: "[System/tool] that handles [impressive scale]"

### Creator/Marketing
- Growth stories: "[X followers] in [timeframe]"
- Revenue transparency: "[$X] from [method]"
- Behind-the-scenes: "What [number] viral posts taught me"

### Leadership/Career
- Transformation: "From [role] to [better role]"
- Lessons learned: "[Time] in [position] revealed [insight]"
- Hiring/team: "I've interviewed [number] candidates. Here's what matters:"

## Re-Hook Strategy

**After the main hook, add a "re-hook" to maintain interest**:

Main hook: "If I had to learn Product Management again, I would start here."
Re-hook: "($2,000 of curated resources, completely free)"

Main hook: "Meet the billionaire who never spent a dollar on marketing."
Re-hook: "His secret? Word of mouth at scale."

**Re-hook purposes**:
- Adds additional curiosity
- Provides extra value proposition
- Reinforces emotional appeal
- Can use parentheses for bonus info

## Real Example Breakdowns

### Example 1: High Information + Emotion
"Meet America's first hot sauce billionaire: a Vietnamese refugee who built a $150M per year empire with no marketing or sales team."

**Why it worked**:
- "Meet" creates personal connection
- Specific descriptor: "first hot sauce billionaire"
- Emotional element: "Vietnamese refugee"
- Impressive numbers: "$150M per year"
- Counterintuitive detail: "no marketing or sales team"
- Multiple audience appeals

**Character count**: ~170 (uses full hook space)

### Example 2: Learning Journey + Value
"If I had to learn AI Product Management again, I would start here. (Complete roadmap, free)"

**Why it works**:
- Popular template: learning journey
- Timely topic: AI + PM
- Value add: "complete roadmap"
- Free hook: creates urgency
- Short and punchy

**Character count**: ~104 (leaves room for expansion)

**Monitor for trending topics to combine with proven templates**:

- Major industry news
- Seasonal business cycles (Q4, new year, etc.)
- Cultural moments
- Platform algorithm changes

**Quick response framework**:
1. Identify trending topic
2. Select proven template
3. Add unique angle/data
4. Ship within 24-48 hours of trend peak
